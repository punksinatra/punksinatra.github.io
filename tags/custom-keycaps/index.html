TX-CP with Maxkeys Ashen R1

I traded my Modern Beige Purple Hangul PBT for a set of Maxkeys Ashen R1. I wanted to see what the fuss was about regarding maxkeys aside from of course the price and that it comes fully sculptured 1-1-2-3-4-3.

I wanted this set because it matches the color scheme of my TX-CP and because of this I might take a look at other keysets using this color scheme (SA Honeywell maybe?)

I can confirm that the complaints people had on reddit and other mechanical keyboard forums are correct. Beforehand I know about the “Back Space” “SHIF T” issues they had and I can live with that but what bothers me are the following:

  • Inconsistent Legends
  • Non-centered legends

Those are the things that bothers me the most.. I mean this set the whole number row is not centered and the “1 !” key is bolder than the other keys.

They also feel more hollow compared to Signature Platics keys (I know since I owned SA keycaps until now)

Well for the price basically you get what you pay for

Doubleshot PBT in Carbon Colors

Yep you heard the title right. It seems like China is upping their keycap game with this new achievement.

For the most part of my mechanical keyboard life, many have asked and have wondered if it was possible to produce doubleshot PBT keycaps. 1 for one like the feel of PBT dyesubbed keycaps because of its thickness and also I like how it feels when I put my fingers on it.

Many (including signature plastics) have said that it was very hard to produce doubleshot PBT keycaps resulting to multiple dye subbed keycap sets. A chinese manufacturer may prove them wrong this time with these set. I believe it is the first one in cherry profile too.

Price is also much more affordable compared to GMK or SP doubleshot abs keycaps.

What happens next remains to be seen. Kprepublic the online seller from aliexpress has stated that this is not the first colorway that they intend to produce. Hopefully they produce support for other layouts like 75%, 1800 and ergodox. I still believe once they produce keycaps for the unusual layouts their customer base will grow exponentially.

Modern Biege Purple Hangul

Being in the keyboard game means not only talking/dealing about different keycaps and profiles. It also means dealing with different type of plastics used with keycap manufacturing. Aside from ABS which is the common plastics used with your keyboard or stock keyboard keycaps. There is also PBT which has a different texture and also has different qualities compared to ABS plastic. Common reasons people want to use pbt is because they do not shine as fast as normal ABS plastic, they are not as susceptible to fingerprints or dirt accumulation compared to ABS. If you want to know a lot more about PBT plastic Wikipedia has nice information about it.








This modern beige set that I have came from Originative it is basically like Gateron think pbt or maybe enjoypbt. 1.5mm thick and the feel to it is quite nice. PBT is nice to type on from time to time but if you follow this website you would know that I am more on thick abs specially the Signature Plastics SA profile keycaps. Nonetheless think PBT like these are nice for someone who likes cherry profile keycaps.

JTK Debut PoW (Purple on White)

JTK Debut or sometimes called PoW which is short for purple on white. The first keyset produced by a company JTK. It is like GMK double shot abs. Comparing JTK with GMK, I would say GMK is more polished and the quality is more mature. Not too bad considering JTK is a new company. Not really sure if they created their own molds but if they did then they did a hell of a wonderful job.








I am not really too sure about the history of the molds all I know is that GMK bought the original tooling from Cherry and ever since then they have been producing cherry profile keycaps. In any case these JTK I would say is great if you want to try out doubleshot abs and want to know how it would feel like typing on a GMK.

Hmmmm I am not sure about this combination. I transferred the GMK Carbon TKL set to my Quickfire Stealth with green switches and for some reason I am having a hard time typing fast with good accuracy. I can type fast but I observed that I would need to hit the backspace key more often than I should.

I think I am not for this Cherry profile and clicky switches combination. I did not have this issue when I had the set on my QFR with cherry mx blacks. I moved over the Taihao yellow/blue keycaps to my QFR and another strange thing is I am liking it and I like typing on that thing more than the greens with GMK.

Mechanical Keyboard Keycaps Galore

So this morning while at work I got a call from the courier company that they were at my house front door. I told them no was home and everyone was at work. I asked the guy if he can comeback tomorrow and he said “tah boleh” which is the local term for cannot. I was also pretty hard to communicate with him (I think the feeling is mutual). Eventually he started saying the word “gate” which I guessed he meant was leaving the package inside the gate by throwing it inside. Since I have no other choice as I am bound to work for the next 5 days for 12 hours each day including weekends, I told him yes please throw it inside and I proceeded to give him my ID. If something broke or something was missing i’de be in deepshit.

The mess when I was unpacking and getting ready to try on the keycaps

So enough of the story. With my previous posts I have mentioned that I ordered quite a number of keycaps from Taobao. I wasn’t really expecting too much since the price was way below compared to branded and known companies but the price was still way above my budget but you know how it is. I got 3 sets of TKL and 1 set RGB.
